<objective>
Slice 2 of Phase 5: deliver COLL-02 end-to-end — soft locks as a user-visible affordance — without touching the save path.

Builds on Slice 1's `internal/locks` store. Adds three editor-gated lock endpoints (acquire / force / release) dispatched off the `/pages/*` catch-all by suffix, the `SoftLockBanner` warning component, and the `PageEditor` lock lifecycle: acquire on entering Edit, refresh on a ~30s heartbeat + on save, best-effort release on unmount, render read-only under another session's lock, and a Force edit button that takes over the lock and flips to editable.

The load-bearing UX rule (UI-SPEC): the SoftLockBanner is a *warning* (recoverable — you can force-edit), distinct from the *destructive* conflict surface in Plan 04; and Force edit is presented as "take over editing," never as a promise that your next save is safe.

Purpose: warn a small team before they collide, while keeping the authoritative write-safety control (pages.Save revision check) completely separate and untouched.
Output: handlers_locks.go + router/dispatch wiring + lock client calls + SoftLockBanner + PageEditor lock lifecycle and read-only surface.
</objective>
